Title Clouds Over Public Infrastructure: Rethinking Internet Governance in the Hyperscaler Era
Citation
Credibility Dr. Corinne Cath is a cultural anthropologist who studies the computing industry. She currently works as Head of Global Team Digital at Think-and-Do tank ARTICLE 19. Previously, she did her post-doc on the political economy of cloud computing at the technical university of Delft (NL)
Summary The source describes a case of government services being moved onto tech giant servers in America, explaining that the Dutch migration of its registry to Amazon Web Services could lead to immense national security issues - such as Dutch websites losing accessibility upon corruption of Amazon.

It argues that over-reliant digital infrastructure could face access restrictions if geopolitical tensions shift in American tech giants, and instead promotes localized alternatives to "hyperscaler clouds".

It argues that digital transformation for European countries must not end at creating local replicas or alternatives for big tech services. Instead, it argues that there are harms in the mere act of using cloud for public institutions. Primarily, this is due to the commercial and profit-driven nature of the cloud, and how national institutions may shift their values to align with cloud providers due to hiring their personnel.
