Credibility
Summary - Centralization leads to concerns of weakened resilience - one damaged packet or website can break a vast system and cease operations for long periods of time

Technical factors include an incompatibility and competition over IP address versions IPv4 and IPv6. Due to its costly requirements, companies and web nodes seldom prefer to transition to IPv6 and even those who do retain their IPv4 infrastructure that makes more money from more clients. The problem with IPv4 is IPv4 exhaustion, which has already happened, the exceeding of its capacity in holding IP addresses. Now the IPv4 market is basically a real estate market, with addresses being traded and reused and sold at higher prices each year. With limited IPv4 addresses, people turn to solutions like NAT, where many addresses share one public address while they themselves are hidden. The authors claim that this nature of the NAT system will contribute to fragmentation.
